A Tribute to Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali, born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. (January 17, 1942 â€“ June 3, 2016), was an American professional boxer, activist, and philanthropist. He is widely regarded as one of the most significant and celebrated sports figures of the 20th century.

Ali was known for his incredible boxing skills, agility, and charisma both inside and outside the ring. Ali's fights against his rivals, such as Joe Frazier and George Foreman, are legendary and have become part of boxing history.

Apart from his boxing career, Ali was also an influential civil rights activist and spoke out against racial inequality and injustice. He famously refused to be drafted into the military during the Vietnam War, citing his religious beliefs and opposition to the war. His activism and outspokenness made him an icon for many people around the world.

Muhammad Ali passed away on June 3, 2016, but his legacy continues to inspire and resonate with people of all ages.

He will always be remembered as "The Greatest."
